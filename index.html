Orthodontic Software Interoperability

Unleashing the potential of orthodontic software

Conflict of Interest

  • Department of Orthodontics at Case
  • American Dental Association Standards Committee for Dental Informatics
  • tops Software

Presentation Goals

  • Raise awareness in topic of Interoperability
  • Become part of AAO mission statement.

The Problem

  • Orthodontic Softwares don't communicate with each other easily
  • Providers don't know exactly how to share data between each other
  • Providers don't know how to give patients their data

The Problem

  • A lot of time and resources spend for data conversion (import and export)
  • Data conversion is a tedious repetitive process: developers keep reverse engineering and don't share their work.

Strategic Plan

How can the AAO get involved

  • Require anyone that applies for AAO/AAOF funding take Interoperability into consideration.
  • Regularly publish articles to educate domain on Interoperability.

Interoperability Guidelines

  1. A developer should always make use of existing standards
  2. If there are no standards, a developer is expected to invent their own standard.
  3. When a new standard is invented, it shall be made public, and eventually adopted by and SDO.
  • A developer should always make use of an existing, public, open, machine-readable and recognized standard format for exporting, importing and interchanging all of the data. (The intention is to prevent developers from holding on to the data the user has added).
  • If a developer is developing a novel product, for which no such standard exists yet, the developer can invent their own format, as long as it is made public, and open for usage by anyone. (The intention is to allow technological innovation, while maintaining interoperability). In such circumstance:
    • the developer shall facilitate/promote the standardization of this format through a recognized SDO. (The intention is that this new format becomes official and implemented by others as well)
    • if a new versions of the format is necessary, the developer shall maintain backwards compatibility with the previously used format, either directly (within the new version of the product itself), or by developing and making publicly available open-source conversion tools.
    • in any event, any subsequent format changes and revisions shall be made publicly available for usage, implementation and development, just as the first one. (The intention is to prevent possible abuse of this regulation by making the first format public and open, but then making revisions closed and proprietary)

Ask you software's support these questions

Find out if you really have control over your data.

  • The day I decide to move to a different software product, what will the process to export my data and import it into the new software be?
  • I would like to use Software B to send out my email reminders. Can you please ensure the two software products can share data both ways?
